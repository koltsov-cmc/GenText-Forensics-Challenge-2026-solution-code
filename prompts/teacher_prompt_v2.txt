RULES FOR STUDENT (cases A–J — same rules the student will follow at inference time)
================================================================

CASES STRONGLY FAVOURING KEEP:

A. **Region inside a paragraph or between words.** A DTD region sitting inside running prose or between words, with surrounding clean text, is very likely tampered text. → most often **Visual Clumsy** if the artefact is visible, **Semantic Subtle** if the substituted word is visually clean but contextually wrong, **Logical Fraud** if a numeric value was changed.

B. **Blurred / JPEG-distorted text.** Blur, smearing, or heavy JPEG-compression artefacts inside a DTD region. → **Visual Clumsy**.

C. **Solid rectangle / white-out / black-out.** Solid coloured rectangles, white empty patches, black/grey bars over content. → **Visual Clumsy**.

D. **Empty area in the middle of running text.** A clear empty gap that breaks the natural flow of words. → **Visual Clumsy** or **Logical Fraud** (removed value).

E. **Blurred logo / stamp / seal** while surrounding document is sharp. → **Visual Clumsy**.

F. **Numeric value where the digits look clean.** Tightly-bounded number, date, percentage, total, page number, or statistic, no visible artefact. → **Logical Fraud**.

G. **Single word or short phrase, visually clean, in running prose** that contradicts surrounding context. → **Semantic Subtle**.

DROP RULES (the only valid reasons to remove a DTD region):

H. **Tiny, weak, irregular shape**, mostly blue-green on the heatmap rather than red/yellow, AND not approximately rectangular. → DTD noise, drop.

I. **In document margins / blank borders, far from any content,** AND visually empty. → low-quality false positive, drop.

J. **Math content in a math-heavy document with massive DTD activation,** AND the formula renders cleanly. → DTD misfiring on math glyph statistics, drop.

HARD RULES FOR YOU (THE TEACHER)
================================================================

1. **Never reveal that you have privileged information.** Forbidden phrases include but are not limited to: "given the answer", "since we know", "the GT says", "according to the ground truth", "the mask shows", "the answer is", "we already know", "as revealed", "it is given that", "the TP/FP label". 2. **Coordinate strategy for Stage 4 — simple 3-step algorithm.** For each anomaly in "GROUND-TRUTH TARGET REPORT", find its `[GROUNDING]` bbox in this order:

   **Step 1.** Look at the mask region for this anomaly (from "PRECISE TAMPERING REGIONS FROM GT MASK"). Check if any DTD region labelled **TP** overlaps this mask region. If yes → **use the DTD bbox AS-IS** as `[GROUNDING]`. Done.

   **Step 2.** If no TP-DTD region matches this mask (DTD missed this anomaly, or only an FP-DTD region happens to be in the area) → find the OCR item(s) whose bboxes intersect the mask region. **Use that OCR bbox** as `[GROUNDING]`. If the tampered content spans multiple adjacent OCR items, use the **union** of their bboxes. Done.

   **Step 3.** If neither a TP-DTD region nor any OCR item intersects the mask (rare: blank-area redactions with no extractable text) → **use the mask bbox itself** as `[GROUNDING]`. Done.

   **Hard constraints:**
   * **NEVER use an FP-DTD bbox as `[GROUNDING]`.** FP-DTD regions have wrong coordinates by definition.
   * **NEVER copy mask coordinates when Step 1 or Step 2 produced a result.** Mask coordinates are often imprecise (a fragment of a word or stretching slightly past it); only fall through to mask in Step 3.
   * **Always prefer DTD-TP over OCR when DTD-TP exists for the mask region.** DTD-TP coordinates have been validated by the mask overlap; the student should learn to trust them.

   **Worked examples:**

   *Example A — DTD-TP wins:* Mask region is `[2245, 311, 2325, 344]` covering the policy number "9.99". DTD Region 1 is `[2244, 310, 2335, 346]` labelled TP. → `[GROUNDING]: [2244, 310, 2335, 346]` (DTD).

   *Example B — OCR wins (DTD missed entirely):* Mask region covers part of "Writing"; no DTD region overlaps. OCR item 87 for "Writing" is `[450, 980, 620, 1020]`. → `[GROUNDING]: [450, 980, 620, 1020]` (OCR).

   *Example C — OCR wins (DTD overlaps but is FP):* Mask region covers "96%". A DTD region overlaps the area but is labelled FP (its bbox has poor shape — only a fragment or wrong slice). OCR item 51 for "96%" is `[1370, 1100, 1469, 1138]`. → `[GROUNDING]: [1370, 1100, 1469, 1138]` (OCR). Never the FP-DTD bbox.

   *Example D — mask wins (last resort):* Mask covers a solid white rectangle where text was erased. No DTD region overlaps and no OCR item is present (blank area). → `[GROUNDING]: <mask coordinates verbatim>`.

   This 3-step algorithm is the entire coordinate logic. Apply it mechanically: check Step 1, then Step 2, then Step 3.

3. **Walk through every DTD region in Stage 2.** Use the privileged TP/FP labels to set the verdict:
   * **If a region is labelled TP — it MUST be KEEP.** Do not drop a TP-labelled region just because the visual evidence looks clean. A clean-looking region that is TP is exactly the signature of **Semantic Subtle** (word substitution with no visual artefact) or **Logical Fraud** (value altered but digits render fine). When TP label and visual cleanness disagree, the resolution is "this is Semantic Subtle / Logical Fraud", NOT "this is FP".
   * **If a region is labelled FP — it MUST be DROP.** Justify with one of rules H / I / J or a one-sentence visual reason.
   * Justify every verdict in terms inferrable from image / heatmap / OCR / GT-target context. Never write "this is TP" or "the label says".

4. **Stage 3 finds anomalies present in "GROUND-TRUTH TARGET REPORT" that did NOT end up in your Stage 2 KEEP list.** This includes:
   * anomalies where DTD did not flag any region at all (purely-semantic discoveries),
   * anomalies where DTD flagged a region but you correctly classified the surrounding semantics — these may need extra context in Stage 3 even if the bbox came from Stage 2.
   For each Stage 3 anomaly, re-derive it from semantic/logical reasoning over the OCR text. Articulate the contradiction. Do NOT invent anomalies beyond what is in the target report.

5. **Coverage check.** The total number of distinct anomalies you describe across Stage 2 (KEEPs) and Stage 3 MUST equal the number of anomalies in "GROUND-TRUTH TARGET REPORT". If you find that some target anomalies are missing from your combined list, you have either wrongly dropped a TP region in Stage 2 (fix it) or missed a Stage 3 semantic anomaly (find and add it).
