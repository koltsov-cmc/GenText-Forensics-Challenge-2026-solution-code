RULES FOR STUDENT
================================================================
A. **Region inside a paragraph or between words.** A DTD region sitting inside running prose or between words, with surrounding clean text, is very likely tampered text. Look closely: there will usually be a slight font, weight, baseline, or kerning inconsistency relative to neighbouring text. → most often **Visual Clumsy** if the artefact is visible, **Semantic Subtle** if the substituted word is visually clean but contextually wrong, **Logical Fraud** if a numeric value was changed.

B. **Blurred / JPEG-distorted text.** Blur, smearing, or heavy JPEG-compression artefacts inside a DTD region are deliberate manipulation attempts to obscure content. → **Visual Clumsy**.

C. **Solid rectangle / white-out / black-out.** Solid coloured rectangles, white empty patches, black/grey bars over content are redactions. → **Visual Clumsy**.

D. **Empty area in the middle of running text.** A clear empty gap that breaks the natural flow of words inside a paragraph or sentence indicates someone deleted text. → **Visual Clumsy** (if the gap itself is visible) or sometimes a marker for a removed value (**Logical Fraud**).

E. **Blurred logo / stamp / seal.** A logo, stamp, or seal that is blurred, smudged, partially erased, or visibly retouched while the surrounding document is sharp — a deliberate attempt to obscure the issuing institution. → **Visual Clumsy**.

F. **Numeric value where the digits look clean.** A DTD region tightly bounding a number, date, percentage, total, page number, or statistic, with no visible visual artefact. The digits themselves may be perfectly rendered, but the VALUE is the tampering. → **Logical Fraud**.

G. **Single word or short phrase, visually clean, in running prose.** A DTD region tightly bounding one or two words in body text, where the rendering looks fine but the word makes the local context contradictory. → **Semantic Subtle**.

DROP RULES (the only valid reasons to remove a DTD region):

H. **Tiny, weak, irregular shape, on the heat map mostly blue-green rather than red/yellow, AND not approximately rectangular.** Likely DTD noise. Drop.

I. **In document margins / blank borders / far from any text/figure/table content,** AND visually empty (no redaction-like patch). Likely a low-quality false positive. Drop.

J. **Mathematical content in a math-heavy document with massive DTD activation,** AND the formula renders cleanly with no edit artefact. Drop — DTD is misfiring on math glyph statistics.
