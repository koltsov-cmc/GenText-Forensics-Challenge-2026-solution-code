CASES STRONGLY FAVOURING KEEP (when tampering IS present — not the case here):

A. **Region inside a paragraph or between words.** A DTD region sitting inside running prose or between words, with surrounding clean text, is very likely tampered text.

B. **Blurred / JPEG-distorted text.** Blur, smearing, or heavy JPEG-compression artefacts inside a DTD region.

C. **Solid rectangle / white-out / black-out.** Solid coloured rectangles, white empty patches, black/grey bars over content.

D. **Empty area in the middle of running text.** A clear empty gap that breaks the natural flow of words.

E. **Blurred logo / stamp / seal** while surrounding document is sharp.

F. **Numeric value where the digits look clean.** Tightly-bounded number with no visible artefact — usually Logical Fraud.

G. **Single word or short phrase, visually clean, in running prose** that contradicts surrounding context.

DROP RULES (the only valid reasons to remove a DTD region — ALL APPLICABLE HERE):

H. **Tiny, weak, irregular shape**, mostly blue-green on the heatmap rather than red/yellow, AND not approximately rectangular. → DTD noise, drop.

I. **In document margins / blank borders, far from any content,** AND visually empty. → low-quality false positive, drop.

J. **Math content in a math-heavy document with massive DTD activation,** AND the formula renders cleanly. → DTD misfiring on math glyph statistics, drop.

ADDITIONAL DROP REASONS FOR PRISTINE DOCUMENTS:

K. **Region sits on consistent, well-rendered text** that is grammatically and semantically coherent with the surrounding paragraph. The DTD activation is likely a font-statistics artefact, not real tampering.

L. **Region overlaps a logo, stamp, header rule, or graphic element** that is part of the original layout and shows no editing artefact. DTD frequently misfires on dense graphics.

M. **Region overlaps a numeric value that is consistent with the document's own internal logic** (date matches header, total matches line-items, percentage is plausible). DTD's statistical signal does not imply tampering when the value is logically valid.
